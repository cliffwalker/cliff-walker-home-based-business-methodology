# Governance, Ethics & Responsible Use

This document defines the governance principles, ethical standards, and usage boundaries for the **Cliff Walker Home-Based Business Methodology**.

It exists to protect the integrity of the methodology, ensure responsible use, and provide clarity around accountability, version control, and acceptable application.

---

## Purpose of Governance

The governance framework exists to:

- Preserve the integrity and intent of the methodology  
- Prevent misuse, misrepresentation, or misleading claims  
- Ensure ethical and responsible application  
- Support long-term consistency and version control  
- Enable structured evolution of the framework over time  

This methodology is designed as an educational and operational framework, not as a guarantee of outcomes.

---

## Core Governance Principles

### 1. Ethical Practice
All use of this methodology must align with ethical, lawful, and transparent business conduct.

This includes:
- Honest communication  
- No deceptive or manipulative practices  
- No exaggerated or misleading claims  
- Respect for individuals and communities  

---

### 2. No Income or Outcome Guarantees
This methodology does **not** promise or imply:

- Income levels  
- Financial returns  
- Business success  
- Specific performance outcomes  

Results depend on individual effort, skill, context, consistency, and execution.

Any representation suggesting guaranteed or typical outcomes is not permitted.

---

### 3. Responsible Use of AI

Where AI tools are referenced or used in connection with this methodology, they must be applied responsibly.

Principles include:

- AI is a support tool, not a decision-maker  
- Human judgment and accountability remain central  
- Outputs should be reviewed before use  
- AI must not be used to mislead, impersonate, or deceive  
- Privacy and data protection must be respected at all times

---

### 4. Privacy and Data Protection

Users of this methodology are responsible for ensuring compliance with applicable privacy and data protection laws in their jurisdiction.

This includes:
- Respecting personal data  
- Avoiding misuse of private or sensitive information  
- Using secure handling practices  
- Obtaining appropriate consent where required  

This repository does not collect, process, or store personal data.

---

## Version Control & Change Management

To maintain consistency and trust, all updates to this methodology follow structured version control principles.

### Versioning Rules
- All official changes must be recorded in `CHANGELOG.md`
- Major revisions should be released as tagged versions
- Historical versions must remain accessible
- Changes should be documented clearly and transparently

### Change Review
Proposed changes should:
- Clearly identify the section affected  
- Explain the rationale for the change  
- Describe the expected impact  
- Preserve alignment with governance principles  

Unreviewed or informal changes should not be treated as authoritative.

This repository is intended to serve as:
